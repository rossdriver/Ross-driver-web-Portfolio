NC Emissions
project

A dive into NC Greenhouse gas emissions,
from the top green house gas producing plants.

My North Carolina Greenhouse Gas project is a look into around the top 90 emitting plants within North Carolina. The purpose of my research was so I am prepared once I join the workforce and we are looking to source materials from ethical manufacturers. Having a foot in the door on which companies are striving harder to control their greenhouse gas emissions can help align my future employers with ethical standing companies.

The biggest takeaway from my project was the upper outliers. Around the top 15% were each polluting as much as the bottom 85 plants added together. This became the focal point of my research, looking into the gap between the top 15% compared to everyone else. Lots of my data showed centralization of many of the plants. This conclusion makes me curious as to future research of the air quality in the specific centralized zones. Above is a chart showing the distribution of how many plants are in a single county.
